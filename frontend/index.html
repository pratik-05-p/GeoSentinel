Input Parameters

Enter these seven geological and environmental indicators to generate a comprehensive landslide risk assessment for any site.

📐

Slope Angle

Terrain inclination in degrees (0°–90°). The dominant driver of gravitational stress on slope material. Higher angles significantly increase instability.

Weight: 25%
🌧

Rainfall Intensity

Average or peak precipitation in mm/h (0–1000 mm/h). High rainfall increases pore-water pressure, which is the primary triggering mechanism for most landslides.

Weight: 20%
🪨

Soil Type

Dominant soil classification: Clay, Silt, Loam, Sandy, or Rocky. Clay soils have very low cohesion when saturated and are the most susceptible to failure.

Weight: 18%
🌿

Vegetation Cover

Percentage of area covered by vegetation (0–100%). Root systems reinforce soil and reduce runoff. Higher coverage is a stabilising factor that lowers the hazard score.

Weight: 7% (stabilising)
⚡

Fault Zone Proximity

Distance in km to the nearest known geological fault (0–500 km). Sites close to active faults are pre-conditioned for slope failure through seismic activity and fractured rock.

Weight: 10%
🏙

Land Use Type

Current land classification: Urban, Bare Ground, Agricultural, or Forest. Urban and bare ground surfaces increase runoff and surcharge loading compared to forested areas.

Weight: 5%
💧

Drainage Condition

Quality of site drainage: Good, Moderate, or Poor. Poor drainage concentrates water at the slope base and amplifies the destabilising effect of rainfall intensity.

Weight: 15%

What You Get

Every assessment produces four types of output designed to support decision-making at every level — from field surveys to formal reports.

📊

Hazard Score

A numeric value from 0 to 100 representing computed landslide susceptibility, displayed on an animated arc gauge.

🏷

Risk Category

One of four colour-coded labels: Low, Moderate, High, or Critical.

🔍

Risk Explanation

Factor-level breakdown showing which parameters increased risk (▲), reduced risk (▼), or were neutral (→), with a plain-language summary.

🛠

Recommendations

Tailored mitigation actions — from improving drainage to restricting construction — generated from your specific parameter combination.

Risk Category Reference

The hazard score is mapped to one of four risk categories to provide an immediately interpretable result.

Low Score 0–24

Minimal susceptibility. Standard monitoring is sufficient. No immediate intervention required.

Moderate Score 25–49

Elevated susceptibility. Review drainage and vegetation. Periodic site inspection recommended.

High Score 50–74

Significant susceptibility. Geotechnical survey recommended. Consider engineering controls.

Critical Score 75–100

Extreme susceptibility. Immediate expert assessment required. Restrict construction activity.
